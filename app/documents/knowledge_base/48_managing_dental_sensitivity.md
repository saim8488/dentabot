# Managing Dental Sensitivity

## What Is Tooth Sensitivity?

Tooth sensitivity (dentin hypersensitivity) is a common condition that causes a sharp, temporary pain in one or more teeth when exposed to certain triggers. It occurs when the inner layer of the tooth (dentin) becomes exposed due to enamel wear, gum recession, or other factors.

## Common Triggers

- Hot or cold foods and beverages
- Sweet or acidic foods
- Cold air
- Brushing or flossing
- Alcohol-based mouthwashes

## Causes of Sensitivity

- Worn tooth enamel from aggressive brushing or acidic diet
- Gum recession exposing the tooth root
- Tooth decay or cracked teeth
- Recent dental procedures (temporary sensitivity)
- Teeth grinding (bruxism)
- Whitening products

## How to Manage Sensitivity

- **Use Desensitizing Toothpaste:** Special toothpastes containing potassium nitrate or stannous fluoride can block pain signals over time.
- **Brush Gently:** Use a soft-bristled toothbrush and avoid aggressive scrubbing.
- **Avoid Acidic Foods:** Limit citrus fruits, sodas, and vinegar-based foods that can erode enamel.
- **Wear a Night Guard:** If you grind your teeth, a custom night guard can protect your enamel.
- **Use Fluoride Rinse:** Fluoride strengthens enamel and reduces sensitivity.

## When to See Your Dentist

If sensitivity persists or worsens, schedule an appointment. Your dentist can identify the underlying cause and may recommend professional treatments such as fluoride varnish, dental bonding, or gum graft surgery.
