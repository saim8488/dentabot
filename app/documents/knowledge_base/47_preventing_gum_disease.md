# Preventing Gum Disease

## What Is Gum Disease?

Gum disease is an infection of the tissues that hold your teeth in place. It begins as gingivitis (inflammation of the gums) and can progress to periodontitis, which damages the bone and connective tissue supporting the teeth.

## Warning Signs

- Red, swollen, or tender gums
- Bleeding when brushing or flossing
- Persistent bad breath
- Receding gums (teeth appearing longer)
- Loose teeth or changes in bite alignment

## Prevention Strategies

- **Brush Properly:** Brush twice a day, paying careful attention to the gumline where plaque tends to accumulate.
- **Floss Daily:** Flossing removes plaque from between the teeth and under the gumline where brushing alone cannot reach.
- **Use Antimicrobial Mouthwash:** An antibacterial rinse can help reduce plaque-causing bacteria and support gum health.
- **Quit Tobacco:** Smoking and tobacco use are major risk factors for gum disease and can interfere with healing.
- **Eat Well:** A nutritious diet supports your immune system and helps your body fight infections, including gum disease.
- **Manage Health Conditions:** Conditions like diabetes can increase your risk for gum disease. Keep chronic conditions well-managed.

## Professional Care

Regular dental check-ups and professional cleanings are essential for gum disease prevention. Your dentist can detect early signs of gum disease and intervene before it progresses. Patients with a history of gum disease may need periodontal maintenance every 3 to 4 months.
