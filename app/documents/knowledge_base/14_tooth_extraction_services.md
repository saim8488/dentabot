# Tooth Extraction Services

## When Is Tooth Extraction Necessary?

Tooth extraction is the removal of a tooth from its socket in the jawbone. It may be recommended when a tooth is severely decayed, damaged beyond repair, affected by advanced gum disease, or causing overcrowding that interferes with orthodontic treatment.

## Types of Extractions

- **Simple Extraction:** Performed on a tooth that is visible above the gumline. The dentist loosens the tooth with an instrument called an elevator and removes it with dental forceps.
- **Surgical Extraction:** Required for teeth that are broken at the gumline, impacted, or not fully erupted. A small incision is made in the gum to access and remove the tooth.

## The Extraction Process

1. The area is numbed with local anesthesia. Sedation options are available for anxious patients.
2. The tooth is carefully loosened and removed.
3. Gauze is placed over the extraction site to control bleeding and encourage clot formation.
4. Post-operative instructions are provided.

## Aftercare Tips

- Bite gently on the gauze for 30–45 minutes after the procedure.
- Avoid drinking through a straw for 24 hours to prevent dislodging the blood clot.
- Eat soft foods and avoid hot, spicy, or crunchy foods for the first few days.
- Take prescribed or over-the-counter pain medication as directed.
- Contact the clinic if you experience excessive bleeding, severe pain, or signs of infection.
