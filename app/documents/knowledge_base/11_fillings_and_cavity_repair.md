# Fillings and Cavity Repair

## What Are Dental Fillings?

Dental fillings are used to restore teeth damaged by decay (cavities). The decayed portion of the tooth is removed, and the area is filled with a durable material to restore the tooth's shape, function, and strength.

## Types of Filling Materials

- **Composite Resin:** Tooth-colored fillings that blend naturally with your teeth. Ideal for visible areas.
- **Amalgam:** Silver-colored fillings made from a mix of metals. Known for their durability, especially in back teeth.
- **Ceramic/Porcelain:** Tooth-colored and highly resistant to staining. Often used for inlays and onlays.
- **Glass Ionomer:** Releases fluoride to help protect the tooth. Commonly used for fillings below the gumline or in children.

## The Filling Procedure

1. The dentist numbs the area around the affected tooth with a local anesthetic.
2. The decayed tissue is removed using a dental drill or laser.
3. The cavity is cleaned and prepared.
4. The filling material is placed, shaped, and hardened (often with a curing light for composite fillings).
5. The bite is checked and the filling is polished.

## Aftercare

After a filling, mild sensitivity to hot or cold is normal and usually subsides within a few days. Avoid chewing on the treated side until the anesthetic wears off. Maintain regular brushing and flossing to protect the filling and surrounding tooth.
