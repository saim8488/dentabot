# Dental X-rays and Imaging

## Purpose of Dental X-rays

Dental X-rays (radiographs) are an essential diagnostic tool that allows dentists to see structures not visible during a regular oral examination. They help identify cavities between teeth, bone loss, infections, impacted teeth, and other hidden problems.

## Types of Dental X-rays

- **Bitewing X-rays:** Show the upper and lower back teeth and help detect cavities between teeth and changes in bone density caused by gum disease.
- **Periapical X-rays:** Capture the entire tooth from crown to root and the surrounding bone structure.
- **Panoramic X-rays:** Provide a broad view of the entire mouth, including all teeth, jaws, sinuses, and jaw joints in a single image.
- **Cone Beam CT (CBCT):** A 3D imaging technology used for complex cases such as implant planning, orthodontic assessment, and surgical evaluations.

## Safety and Radiation

Modern digital X-rays use significantly less radiation than traditional film X-rays. We use lead aprons and thyroid collars to protect patients during imaging. The amount of radiation from a dental X-ray is very low and considered safe for routine diagnostic use.

## How Often Are X-rays Needed

The frequency of dental X-rays depends on your oral health, age, risk factors, and symptoms. New patients typically receive a full set of X-rays. Follow-up X-rays are taken as clinically needed, usually every 12 to 24 months for routine monitoring.
