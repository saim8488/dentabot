# Daily Oral Hygiene Routines

## Why Daily Oral Hygiene Matters

A consistent daily oral hygiene routine is the foundation of good dental health. It removes plaque, prevents cavities, protects against gum disease, and keeps your breath fresh.

## Recommended Daily Routine

### Morning
1. Brush your teeth for two minutes using a fluoride toothpaste and a soft-bristled toothbrush.
2. Clean your tongue with your toothbrush or a tongue scraper to remove bacteria.
3. Rinse with an antimicrobial or fluoride mouthwash if recommended by your dentist.

### After Meals
- If possible, rinse your mouth with water after eating to wash away food particles and acids.
- Wait at least 30 minutes after consuming acidic foods or drinks before brushing.

### Before Bed
1. Floss between all teeth to remove plaque and debris from areas your toothbrush cannot reach.
2. Brush your teeth thoroughly for two minutes.
3. Use mouthwash as a final step for added protection.

## Additional Tips

- Replace your toothbrush every 3 to 4 months or sooner if the bristles are frayed.
- Consider using an electric toothbrush for more effective plaque removal.
- Drink plenty of water throughout the day to support saliva production.
- Avoid snacking frequently on sugary or acidic foods.

## Professional Support

Even with excellent home care, professional dental cleanings every six months are essential to remove tartar buildup and catch issues early. Your dentist can personalize your hygiene routine based on your specific needs.
