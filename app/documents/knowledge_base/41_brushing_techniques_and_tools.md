# Brushing Techniques and Tools

## Proper Brushing Technique

Using the correct brushing technique is just as important as brushing regularly. Poor technique can leave plaque behind and even damage your gums and enamel.

## The Modified Bass Technique (Recommended)

1. Hold your toothbrush at a 45-degree angle to the gumline.
2. Use gentle, short back-and-forth strokes or small circular motions.
3. Brush the outer surfaces, inner surfaces, and chewing surfaces of all teeth.
4. For the inside surfaces of front teeth, tilt the brush vertically and use gentle up-and-down strokes.
5. Brush your tongue gently to remove bacteria and freshen breath.
6. Spend at least two minutes brushing—about 30 seconds per quadrant of your mouth.

## Choosing the Right Toothbrush

- **Manual Toothbrush:** Choose one with soft bristles and a head size that fits comfortably in your mouth.
- **Electric Toothbrush:** Oscillating or sonic toothbrushes can be more effective at removing plaque, especially for people with limited dexterity.
- **Bristle Type:** Always choose soft bristles. Medium or hard bristles can wear down enamel and irritate gums.

## Toothpaste

Use a fluoride toothpaste approved by your national dental association. Specialty toothpastes are available for sensitivity, whitening, and gum health. Your dentist can recommend the best option for your needs.

## When to Replace Your Toothbrush

Replace your toothbrush or brush head every 3 to 4 months, or sooner if the bristles become frayed or splayed. Also replace it after recovering from an illness.
