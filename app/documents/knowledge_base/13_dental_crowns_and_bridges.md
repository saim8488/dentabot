# Dental Crowns and Bridges

## What Are Dental Crowns?

A dental crown is a custom-made cap that covers a damaged, weakened, or aesthetically compromised tooth. Crowns restore the tooth's shape, size, strength, and appearance. They are commonly used after root canal treatment, for large cavities, or for cracked teeth.

## What Are Dental Bridges?

A dental bridge replaces one or more missing teeth by anchoring an artificial tooth (pontic) to the adjacent natural teeth or implants. Bridges help restore your bite, prevent remaining teeth from shifting, and improve the appearance of your smile.

## Types of Crowns and Bridges

- **Porcelain/Ceramic:** Natural-looking and ideal for front teeth.
- **Porcelain-Fused-to-Metal (PFM):** Combines strength and aesthetics; suitable for front and back teeth.
- **Zirconia:** Extremely durable and natural in appearance; suitable for all areas of the mouth.
- **Gold Alloy:** Highly durable and long-lasting; often used for back teeth.

## The Procedure

1. The tooth is prepared by reshaping it to accommodate the crown.
2. An impression or digital scan is taken and sent to the dental lab.
3. A temporary crown or bridge is placed while the permanent restoration is fabricated.
4. At the follow-up visit, the permanent crown or bridge is fitted, adjusted, and cemented in place.

## Care and Longevity

With proper oral hygiene and regular check-ups, crowns and bridges can last 10 to 15 years or more. Avoid chewing hard objects like ice or pens, and use floss threaders to clean under bridges.
