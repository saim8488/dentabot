# How a Dental Crown Is Made and Placed

## What Is a Dental Crown?

A dental crown is a tooth-shaped cap placed over a damaged or weakened tooth to restore its shape, size, strength, and appearance. Crowns are commonly used after root canal treatment, for large cavities, cracked teeth, or to improve the look of discolored teeth.

## Step 1: Consultation and Preparation

During your first appointment, the dentist examines the tooth and takes X-rays. The tooth is then reshaped by removing a thin layer of enamel from all sides to create space for the crown. If the tooth is severely damaged, it may need to be built up with filling material first.

## Step 2: Impressions

A digital scan or physical impression of the prepared tooth and surrounding teeth is taken. This is sent to a dental laboratory where your custom crown is fabricated. The process takes about 1 to 2 weeks. In the meantime, a temporary crown is placed to protect the prepared tooth.

## Step 3: Crown Placement

At your second appointment, the temporary crown is removed. The permanent crown is checked for fit, color, and bite alignment. Once everything is confirmed, the crown is permanently cemented onto the tooth.

## Same-Day Crowns

Some clinics, including ours, offer same-day crowns using CAD/CAM technology. A digital scan is taken, and the crown is designed and milled in the office during a single visit, eliminating the need for a temporary crown.

## Materials Used

Crowns can be made from porcelain, ceramic, zirconia, metal alloy, or porcelain-fused-to-metal. Your dentist will recommend the best material based on the tooth's location, function, and your aesthetic preferences.
