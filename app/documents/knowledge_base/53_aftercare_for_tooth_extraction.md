# Aftercare for Tooth Extraction

## Immediately After Extraction

- Bite firmly on the gauze pad placed by your dentist for 30 to 45 minutes to help a blood clot form in the socket.
- Do not spit, rinse vigorously, or use a straw for the first 24 hours, as this can dislodge the blood clot and cause a painful condition called dry socket.
- Avoid touching the extraction site with your tongue or fingers.

## Managing Pain and Swelling

- Take over-the-counter pain relievers (such as ibuprofen) or prescribed medication as directed by your dentist.
- Apply an ice pack to the outside of your cheek for 20 minutes on, 20 minutes off during the first 24 hours to minimize swelling.
- Some swelling and bruising is normal and should subside within a few days.

## Diet and Activities

- Eat soft foods for the first 2 to 3 days: yogurt, applesauce, mashed potatoes, scrambled eggs, and smoothies are good choices.
- Avoid hot, spicy, crunchy, and hard foods that could irritate the extraction site.
- Do not smoke or use tobacco for at least 72 hours, as it significantly delays healing and increases the risk of dry socket.
- Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 to 48 hours.

## Oral Hygiene

- Brush and floss your other teeth normally, but avoid the extraction site.
- After 24 hours, gently rinse with warm salt water several times a day to keep the area clean.

## When to Seek Help

Contact the clinic if you experience excessive bleeding that does not stop, severe pain after 2 to 3 days, fever, pus or foul odor from the extraction site, or numbness that does not resolve.
