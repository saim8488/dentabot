# Mouthwash Use and Recommendations

## What Does Mouthwash Do?

Mouthwash (oral rinse) is a liquid product used to rinse the mouth and can help reduce bacteria, freshen breath, strengthen enamel, and support gum health. It is a supplement to brushing and flossing—not a replacement.

## Types of Mouthwash

- **Antimicrobial/Antibacterial Mouthwash:** Contains ingredients like chlorhexidine or cetylpyridinium chloride that reduce bacteria and help control plaque and gingivitis.
- **Fluoride Mouthwash:** Strengthens tooth enamel and helps prevent cavities. Recommended for patients at higher risk of tooth decay.
- **Cosmetic Mouthwash:** Freshens breath temporarily but does not provide lasting therapeutic benefits.
- **Prescription Mouthwash:** Prescribed by a dentist for specific conditions such as severe gum disease or post-surgical care.

## How to Use Mouthwash

1. Pour the recommended amount (usually 20 mL) into a cup.
2. Swish the liquid around your mouth for 30 to 60 seconds.
3. Gargle briefly if directed.
4. Spit out the mouthwash—do not swallow it.
5. Avoid eating or drinking for 30 minutes after use to maximize effectiveness.

## When to Use Mouthwash

Use mouthwash after brushing and flossing, typically as the last step in your oral hygiene routine. Some dentists recommend using it at a different time from brushing to maximize fluoride exposure from both toothpaste and rinse.

## Recommendations

Ask your dentist which mouthwash is best for your needs. Alcohol-free formulas are available and are gentler on sensitive tissues. Children under 6 should not use mouthwash unless directed by a dentist.
