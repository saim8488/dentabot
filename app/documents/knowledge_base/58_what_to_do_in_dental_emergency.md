# What to Do in a Dental Emergency

## Stay Calm and Act Quickly

Dental emergencies can be stressful, but knowing what to do in the moment can save a tooth and prevent further damage. Here is a quick guide for common dental emergencies.

## Knocked-Out Tooth

1. Pick up the tooth by the crown (the white part). Do not touch the root.
2. Rinse the tooth gently with water if it is dirty. Do not scrub or remove any attached tissue.
3. Try to reinsert the tooth into the socket and hold it in place by gently biting on a clean cloth.
4. If reinsertion is not possible, place the tooth in a container of milk, saliva, or saline solution.
5. Get to the dentist within 30 minutes for the best chance of saving the tooth.

## Severe Toothache

- Rinse your mouth with warm salt water.
- Gently floss around the tooth to remove any trapped food.
- Take an over-the-counter pain reliever as directed (do not place aspirin directly on the gum).
- Apply a cold compress to the outside of the cheek if there is swelling.
- Call the clinic to schedule an urgent appointment.

## Broken or Chipped Tooth

- Rinse your mouth with warm water.
- Save any broken pieces.
- Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling.
- Cover any sharp edges with dental wax or sugar-free gum to protect your tongue and cheeks.
- See your dentist as soon as possible.

## Lost Filling or Crown

- If a crown falls off, try to slip it back over the tooth temporarily using denture adhesive or toothpaste (not super glue).
- Avoid chewing on the affected side.
- Schedule an appointment promptly to have it recemented.
