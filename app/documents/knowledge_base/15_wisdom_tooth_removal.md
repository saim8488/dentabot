# Wisdom Tooth Removal

## What Are Wisdom Teeth?

Wisdom teeth are the third set of molars that typically emerge between the ages of 17 and 25. Many people do not have enough space in their jaw to accommodate these teeth, which can lead to impaction, crowding, pain, or infection.

## Signs You May Need Removal

- Pain or swelling at the back of the mouth
- Difficulty opening your mouth fully
- Red or swollen gums around the wisdom tooth area
- Bad breath or an unpleasant taste due to infection
- Damage to neighboring teeth caused by pressure from the wisdom tooth

## The Removal Procedure

1. **Evaluation:** X-rays or a panoramic image assess the position and condition of the wisdom teeth.
2. **Anesthesia:** Local anesthesia, sedation, or general anesthesia is administered based on the complexity of the case and patient preference.
3. **Extraction:** The dentist or oral surgeon makes an incision if the tooth is impacted, removes bone if necessary, and extracts the tooth. The site is cleaned and sutured if needed.

## Recovery

Recovery typically takes a few days to a week. Swelling and mild discomfort are normal. Apply ice packs to reduce swelling, eat soft foods, and follow all post-operative instructions. Avoid smoking and vigorous rinsing during the healing period. Follow-up visits may be scheduled to monitor healing and remove stitches.
