# Teeth Whitening Safety and Results

## Is Teeth Whitening Safe?

Professional teeth whitening performed by a qualified dentist is a safe and well-established cosmetic procedure. The whitening agents used in dental offices are regulated and applied under controlled conditions to minimize risks.

## Potential Side Effects

- **Tooth Sensitivity:** The most common side effect. It is usually mild and temporary, resolving within a few days after treatment. Desensitizing toothpaste can help.
- **Gum Irritation:** Mild irritation of the gums may occur if the whitening gel contacts soft tissue. Protective barriers are used to minimize this risk during in-office treatments.
- **Uneven Results:** Existing dental restorations (fillings, crowns, veneers) do not respond to whitening. Your dentist will discuss this during your consultation.

## Who Should Avoid Whitening?

- Children under 16 years of age
- Pregnant or nursing women
- Patients with severe tooth sensitivity or gum disease (should be treated first)
- Individuals with allergies to peroxide-based products

## Expected Results

Professional whitening can lighten teeth by several shades in a single session. Results vary based on the original shade, type of staining, and the whitening method used. In-office treatments generally produce more dramatic and immediate results than take-home kits.

## Maintaining Your Results

- Avoid stain-causing beverages (coffee, tea, red wine) for at least 48 hours after treatment.
- Maintain good oral hygiene with regular brushing and flossing.
- Schedule periodic touch-up treatments as recommended by your dentist.
- Results typically last 6 months to 2 years depending on lifestyle and habits.
