# Root Canal Treatment

## What Is a Root Canal?

A root canal is a dental procedure used to treat infection or damage deep inside a tooth. It involves removing the infected or inflamed pulp (the soft tissue inside the tooth), cleaning and disinfecting the root canals, and sealing the tooth to prevent further infection.

## When Is a Root Canal Needed?

Root canal treatment may be necessary if you experience:

- Severe toothache or persistent pain
- Prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold
- Darkening or discoloration of the tooth
- Swelling or tenderness in the nearby gums
- A recurring pimple (abscess) on the gums

## The Root Canal Procedure

1. **Anesthesia:** The area is numbed with local anesthesia for comfort.
2. **Access:** A small opening is made in the crown of the tooth to reach the pulp chamber.
3. **Cleaning:** The infected pulp tissue is carefully removed, and the canals are cleaned and shaped.
4. **Filling:** The canals are filled with a biocompatible material called gutta-percha and sealed.
5. **Restoration:** A crown or filling is placed on the tooth to restore its function and appearance.

## Recovery and Success

Most patients experience relief from pain shortly after the procedure. Mild discomfort for a few days is normal and can be managed with over-the-counter pain relievers. Root canal treatments have a high success rate and can preserve your natural tooth for many years.
