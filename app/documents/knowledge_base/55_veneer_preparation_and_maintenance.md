# Veneer Preparation and Maintenance

## Preparing for Veneers

Before getting veneers, your dentist will conduct a thorough examination to ensure you are a good candidate. This includes checking for gum disease, decay, and sufficient enamel. A treatment plan is created based on your smile goals.

## The Preparation Process

1. **Tooth Reshaping:** A thin layer of enamel (about 0.5 mm) is removed from the front surface of each tooth receiving a veneer. This creates space for the veneer to sit flush with adjacent teeth.
2. **Impressions:** A digital scan or physical mold of your prepared teeth is taken and sent to a dental lab.
3. **Temporary Veneers:** Temporary veneers may be placed while your permanent veneers are being fabricated (typically 1 to 2 weeks).
4. **Bonding:** The final veneers are checked for fit, shape, and color, then permanently bonded to your teeth using dental cement and cured with a special light.

## Caring for Your Veneers

- Brush and floss daily as you would with natural teeth.
- Use a non-abrasive fluoride toothpaste.
- Avoid biting directly into very hard foods (ice, hard candy, bones) with your front teeth.
- Do not use your teeth as tools to open packages or bite nails.
- Wear a mouthguard if you grind your teeth at night or play contact sports.

## Longevity

With proper care, porcelain veneers typically last 10 to 20 years. Composite veneers may last 5 to 7 years. Regular dental check-ups allow your dentist to monitor the condition of your veneers and address any issues early.

## Replacement

Veneers may eventually need to be replaced due to normal wear, chipping, or changes in the surrounding teeth. Your dentist will advise you when it is time for a replacement.
