# Preventing Tooth Decay

## What Causes Tooth Decay?

Tooth decay (dental caries) occurs when bacteria in the mouth produce acids that attack tooth enamel. These bacteria thrive on sugars and starches from food, forming a sticky film called plaque. Over time, repeated acid attacks break down the enamel and create cavities.

## How to Prevent Tooth Decay

- **Brush Twice Daily:** Use fluoride toothpaste and a soft-bristled brush for at least two minutes each time.
- **Floss Daily:** Clean between teeth where plaque accumulates and your toothbrush cannot reach.
- **Use Fluoride:** Fluoride strengthens enamel and helps reverse early stages of decay. Use fluoride toothpaste and ask your dentist about fluoride treatments.
- **Limit Sugary Foods and Drinks:** Reduce consumption of candy, soda, juice, and other sugary items. When you do consume them, rinse your mouth with water afterward.
- **Eat a Balanced Diet:** Choose foods that support dental health, such as fruits, vegetables, dairy, and lean proteins.

## Dental Sealants

Dental sealants are a thin protective coating applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth (molars), where decay most often occurs. Sealants are especially recommended for children and teens but can benefit adults as well.

## Regular Dental Visits

Visit your dentist every six months for check-ups and professional cleanings. Early detection of decay allows for simpler, less costly treatment. Your dentist can identify risk factors and recommend preventive strategies tailored to your needs.
