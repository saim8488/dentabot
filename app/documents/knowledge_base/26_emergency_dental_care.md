# Emergency Dental Care

## What Counts as a Dental Emergency?

A dental emergency is any situation involving severe pain, bleeding, trauma, or infection that requires immediate attention. Common dental emergencies include:

- Severe or uncontrollable toothache
- Knocked-out (avulsed) tooth
- Cracked, broken, or fractured tooth
- Lost filling or crown
- Abscess or swelling in the gums or face
- Soft tissue injuries (cuts to the lips, tongue, or cheeks)
- Jaw injury or dislocation

## What to Do in an Emergency

- **Knocked-Out Tooth:** Handle the tooth by the crown (not the root), rinse gently with water, and try to reinsert it into the socket. If that is not possible, store it in milk or saliva and get to the clinic immediately.
- **Severe Toothache:** Rinse with warm salt water, use dental floss to remove any trapped debris, and take over-the-counter pain relief. Avoid placing aspirin directly on the gum.
- **Broken Tooth:** Rinse your mouth with warm water, apply a cold compress to reduce swelling, and save any tooth fragments.

## Prevention Tips

Wear a mouthguard during sports, avoid chewing ice or hard candy, and do not use your teeth as tools. Regular check-ups help identify vulnerabilities before they become emergencies.
