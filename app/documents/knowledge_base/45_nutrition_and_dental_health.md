# Nutrition and Dental Health

## The Link Between Diet and Oral Health

What you eat and drink has a direct impact on the health of your teeth and gums. A balanced diet provides essential nutrients that strengthen teeth, support gum tissue, and help the body fight oral infections.

## Tooth-Friendly Foods

- **Dairy Products:** Milk, cheese, and yogurt are rich in calcium and phosphorus, which strengthen tooth enamel.
- **Crunchy Fruits and Vegetables:** Apples, carrots, and celery stimulate saliva production and help clean teeth naturally.
- **Leafy Greens:** Spinach, kale, and other greens provide calcium, folic acid, and vitamins that support gum health.
- **Lean Proteins:** Fish, chicken, eggs, and nuts provide phosphorus, which helps protect and rebuild tooth enamel.
- **Water:** Drinking water throughout the day rinses away food particles and keeps the mouth hydrated.

## Foods and Drinks to Limit

- Sugary snacks and candies feed bacteria that produce acid and cause cavities.
- Soda, energy drinks, and fruit juices are high in sugar and acid, which erode enamel.
- Sticky foods like dried fruit, caramel, and gummy candy cling to teeth and are harder to clean.
- Starchy foods like chips and white bread can break down into sugars that feed plaque bacteria.

## Eating Habits That Help

- Eat meals at regular times rather than snacking frequently.
- Rinse your mouth with water after eating acidic or sugary foods.
- Chew sugar-free gum after meals to stimulate saliva and neutralize acids.
- Wait 30 minutes after eating acidic foods before brushing to avoid enamel damage.
