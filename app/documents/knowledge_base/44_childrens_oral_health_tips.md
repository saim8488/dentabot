# Children's Oral Health Tips

## Starting Early

Good oral health habits should begin early in life. Caring for your child's teeth from infancy sets the stage for a lifetime of healthy smiles.

## Infant and Toddler Care (0–3 Years)

- Wipe your baby's gums with a clean, damp cloth after feedings, even before teeth appear.
- Once the first tooth erupts, brush it gently with a soft infant toothbrush and a rice-grain-sized amount of fluoride toothpaste.
- Avoid putting your baby to bed with a bottle of milk or juice, which can cause baby bottle tooth decay.
- Schedule your child's first dental visit by age 1 or within six months of the first tooth.

## Preschool and School-Age Children (3–12 Years)

- Use a pea-sized amount of fluoride toothpaste starting at age 3.
- Supervise brushing until around age 7 to ensure proper technique and adequate time.
- Introduce flossing once teeth begin to touch each other.
- Encourage healthy snacking—limit sugary drinks, candy, and sticky foods.
- Ask your dentist about dental sealants to protect the chewing surfaces of permanent molars.

## Teens

- Continue twice-daily brushing and daily flossing.
- Wear a mouthguard during sports activities.
- Discuss orthodontic options if teeth are misaligned.
- Avoid tobacco products and limit consumption of sugary or acidic drinks.

## Making Dental Care Fun

Use colorful toothbrushes, flavored toothpaste, brushing timers, and reward charts to make oral hygiene enjoyable. Lead by example—let your children see you practicing good dental habits.
