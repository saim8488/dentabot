# Flossing and Interdental Cleaning

## Why Flossing Matters

Brushing alone cleans only about 60% of tooth surfaces. Flossing removes plaque and food particles from between the teeth and below the gumline—areas your toothbrush cannot reach. Regular flossing helps prevent cavities between teeth and reduces the risk of gum disease.

## How to Floss Properly

1. Use about 18 inches of floss. Wind most of it around your middle fingers, leaving 1 to 2 inches to work with.
2. Hold the floss taut between your thumbs and index fingers.
3. Gently slide the floss between two teeth using a back-and-forth motion.
4. Curve the floss into a C-shape against one tooth and gently slide it under the gumline.
5. Move the floss up and down against the tooth surface, then repeat on the adjacent tooth.
6. Use a clean section of floss for each gap.

## Interdental Cleaning Alternatives

- **Interdental Brushes:** Small brushes that fit between teeth; ideal for wider gaps or around braces and bridges.
- **Water Flossers:** Use a stream of pulsating water to clean between teeth. Great for people with braces, implants, or dexterity issues.
- **Floss Picks:** Pre-threaded disposable flossers that are convenient for on-the-go use.
- **Soft Picks:** Flexible rubber-tipped picks for gentle interdental cleaning.

## How Often Should You Floss?

Floss at least once a day, ideally before bedtime brushing. Consistency is key—daily flossing is more effective than occasional deep cleaning.
