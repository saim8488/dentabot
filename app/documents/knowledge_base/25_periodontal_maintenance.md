# Periodontal Maintenance

## What Is Periodontal Maintenance?

Periodontal maintenance is a specialized ongoing cleaning program for patients who have been treated for gum disease (periodontitis). It goes beyond routine dental cleaning and is designed to prevent the recurrence or progression of periodontal disease.

## How It Differs from Regular Cleaning

- **Frequency:** Periodontal maintenance is typically scheduled every 3 to 4 months, compared to every 6 months for standard cleanings.
- **Depth:** It includes thorough cleaning above and below the gumline, including scaling and root planing of any areas showing signs of recurring disease.
- **Monitoring:** Gum pocket depths are measured at each visit to track the health of the gum tissue and detect any changes early.

## What to Expect During a Visit

1. Review of your medical and dental history for any changes.
2. Examination of your teeth, gums, and oral tissues.
3. Measurement of periodontal pocket depths.
4. Removal of plaque, tartar, and bacterial toxins from above and below the gumline.
5. Polishing of the teeth.
6. Personalized oral hygiene recommendations.

## Why It Matters

Gum disease is a chronic condition that can be managed but not cured. Without regular periodontal maintenance, pockets can deepen, bacteria can return, and further bone and tissue loss can occur. Consistent maintenance visits are essential to preserving your teeth and overall oral health.
