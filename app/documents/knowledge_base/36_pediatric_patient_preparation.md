# Pediatric Patient Preparation

## Preparing Your Child for a Dental Visit

A positive first experience at the dentist can set the tone for a lifetime of good oral health. Here are tips to help your child feel comfortable and confident before their dental appointment.

## Before the Visit

- **Talk Positively:** Use simple, positive language to describe the dental visit. Avoid words like "pain," "shot," or "drill."
- **Read Books or Watch Videos:** There are many age-appropriate books and videos about visiting the dentist that can help familiarize your child with what to expect.
- **Role Play:** Pretend to be the dentist at home. Let your child sit in a chair while you count their teeth with a toothbrush.
- **Avoid Bribery:** Instead of promising a reward for being brave, frame the visit as a normal, healthy activity.

## What to Bring

- Your child's favorite toy or comfort item
- Any relevant medical records or medication lists
- Insurance card and completed registration forms

## During the Visit

Our pediatric team is trained to work with children of all ages. We use age-appropriate language, show-and-tell techniques, and gentle approaches to make the experience positive. Parents are welcome to stay with their child during the exam.

## After the Visit

Praise your child for their cooperation. Talk about what happened in a positive way, and remind them that dental visits help keep their teeth strong and healthy.
