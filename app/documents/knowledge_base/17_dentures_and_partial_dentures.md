# Dentures and Partial Dentures

## What Are Dentures?

Dentures are removable prosthetic devices designed to replace missing teeth and surrounding tissue. They restore the ability to eat, speak, and smile with confidence. Dentures are custom-made to fit comfortably in your mouth and match the appearance of natural teeth.

## Types of Dentures

- **Complete Dentures:** Replace all teeth in the upper jaw, lower jaw, or both. They rest on the gums and are held in place by suction or denture adhesive.
- **Partial Dentures:** Replace one or several missing teeth. They attach to remaining natural teeth using metal clasps or precision attachments.
- **Immediate Dentures:** Placed on the same day teeth are extracted, allowing you to have teeth during the healing period. They may require adjustments as the gums heal and change shape.
- **Implant-Supported Dentures:** Secured by dental implants for added stability and reduced slippage.

## Getting Fitted for Dentures

The process involves multiple appointments: impressions of your mouth are taken, bite measurements are recorded, and trial dentures are tested for fit and appearance before the final set is fabricated.

## Care and Maintenance

- Remove and rinse dentures after eating.
- Brush dentures daily with a soft denture brush and non-abrasive cleanser.
- Soak dentures overnight in a denture-cleaning solution.
- Handle dentures carefully to avoid dropping or bending them.
- Visit your dentist regularly for fit checks and oral health exams.
