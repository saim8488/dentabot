# Senior Dental Care Support

## Dental Health for Older Adults

As we age, maintaining oral health becomes increasingly important. Seniors face unique dental challenges including dry mouth, gum disease, tooth decay, and the wear and tear of a lifetime of use. Our clinic provides specialized care to address these needs.

## Common Dental Concerns for Seniors

- **Dry Mouth (Xerostomia):** Often a side effect of medications, dry mouth increases the risk of cavities and gum disease. We can recommend saliva substitutes, special rinses, and hydration strategies.
- **Gum Disease:** Seniors are at higher risk for periodontal disease. Regular cleanings and periodontal maintenance are essential.
- **Root Decay:** Exposed tooth roots from gum recession are more vulnerable to decay.
- **Tooth Loss:** Missing teeth can affect nutrition, speech, and quality of life. Dentures, bridges, and implants are effective replacement options.
- **Oral Cancer:** Risk increases with age. We perform oral cancer screenings during routine check-ups.

## Accessibility and Comfort

Our clinic is fully accessible for patients with mobility challenges. We offer wheelchair-friendly treatment rooms, flexible scheduling, and sedation options for patients who need extra support during procedures.

## Coordinating with Medical Providers

We work with your physicians and specialists to ensure that dental treatment is safe and compatible with your overall medical care, especially if you take blood thinners, have diabetes, or have heart conditions.
