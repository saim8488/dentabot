# Dental Implants Overview

## What Are Dental Implants?

Dental implants are titanium posts surgically placed into the jawbone to serve as artificial tooth roots. Once integrated with the bone, they support a crown, bridge, or denture, providing a permanent and natural-looking replacement for missing teeth.

## Benefits of Dental Implants

- Look, feel, and function like natural teeth
- Preserve jawbone and prevent bone loss
- Do not require altering adjacent healthy teeth (unlike bridges)
- Highly durable—can last a lifetime with proper care
- Improve speech, comfort, and confidence

## The Implant Process

1. **Consultation and Planning:** Your dentist evaluates your oral health, takes X-rays or 3D scans, and develops a personalized treatment plan.
2. **Implant Placement:** The titanium post is surgically inserted into the jawbone under local anesthesia or sedation.
3. **Osseointegration:** Over 3 to 6 months, the implant fuses with the jawbone in a process called osseointegration.
4. **Abutment and Crown:** An abutment is attached to the implant, and a custom-made crown is placed on top to complete the restoration.

## Who Is a Candidate?

Most adults with good general health and sufficient jawbone density are candidates for dental implants. Patients who smoke, have uncontrolled diabetes, or have significant bone loss may require additional evaluation or preparatory procedures such as bone grafting.
