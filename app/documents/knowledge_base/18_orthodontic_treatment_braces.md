# Orthodontic Treatment (Braces)

## What Is Orthodontic Treatment?

Orthodontic treatment corrects the alignment of teeth and jaws to improve bite function and aesthetics. Braces are the most common orthodontic appliance, using brackets, wires, and bands to gradually move teeth into proper position.

## Types of Braces

- **Metal Braces:** Traditional stainless-steel brackets and wires. Highly effective and suitable for all types of orthodontic cases.
- **Ceramic Braces:** Tooth-colored or clear brackets that are less noticeable than metal braces while providing similar results.
- **Lingual Braces:** Placed on the inside (tongue side) of the teeth, making them virtually invisible from the outside.
- **Self-Ligating Braces:** Use a specialized clip instead of elastic bands to hold the wire, potentially reducing friction and treatment time.

## Who Can Benefit?

Orthodontic treatment is suitable for children, teens, and adults. It can address crooked teeth, overcrowding, gaps between teeth, overbites, underbites, crossbites, and other bite irregularities.

## Treatment Duration and Follow-up

Treatment typically lasts 12 to 24 months depending on the complexity of the case. Regular adjustment appointments are scheduled every 4 to 8 weeks. After braces are removed, a retainer is provided to maintain the new tooth alignment.

## Caring for Your Braces

Brush after every meal, use floss threaders or orthodontic flossers, avoid sticky and hard foods, and attend all scheduled adjustment appointments to keep treatment on track.
