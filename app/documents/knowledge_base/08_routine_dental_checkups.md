# Routine Dental Check-ups

## Why Regular Check-ups Matter

Routine dental check-ups are essential for maintaining good oral health. They allow your dentist to detect problems early—such as cavities, gum disease, and oral cancer—before they become serious and costly to treat.

## What Happens During a Check-up

- **Visual Examination:** The dentist examines your teeth, gums, tongue, and the inside of your mouth for signs of decay, disease, or abnormalities.
- **X-rays:** If needed, dental X-rays are taken to identify issues beneath the surface, such as impacted teeth or bone loss.
- **Cleaning:** A dental hygienist may perform a professional cleaning to remove plaque and tartar buildup.
- **Oral Cancer Screening:** Your dentist checks for unusual lumps, sores, or discoloration in the mouth and throat.

## How Often Should You Visit

We recommend a dental check-up every six months for most patients. Some individuals with specific conditions, such as gum disease or a high cavity risk, may need more frequent visits as advised by their dentist.

## After Your Check-up

Your dentist will discuss any findings with you and recommend a treatment plan if needed. You will also receive personalized advice on brushing, flossing, and diet to keep your teeth and gums healthy between visits.
