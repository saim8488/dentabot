# Care After Dental Implant Surgery

## Immediate Post-Surgery Care (First 24–48 Hours)

- **Bleeding:** Light bleeding is normal. Bite gently on the gauze pad placed over the surgical site for 30 to 45 minutes. Replace as needed.
- **Swelling:** Apply an ice pack to the outside of your cheek in 20-minute intervals to reduce swelling. Swelling typically peaks on the second or third day.
- **Rest:** Avoid strenuous physical activity for at least 48 hours. Keep your head elevated when lying down.
- **Diet:** Eat soft, cool foods such as yogurt, smoothies, mashed potatoes, and soup. Avoid hot, spicy, crunchy, or hard foods.

## Oral Hygiene After Surgery

- Do not brush the surgical site for the first 24 hours. Gently brush the rest of your teeth.
- After 24 hours, rinse gently with warm salt water (half a teaspoon of salt in a cup of warm water) several times a day.
- Avoid using a straw, spitting forcefully, or smoking, as these can dislodge the blood clot and delay healing.

## Medications

- Take prescribed antibiotics as directed to prevent infection.
- Use over-the-counter or prescribed pain medication to manage discomfort.
- Do not take aspirin, as it can increase bleeding.

## Healing and Follow-Up

The implant site typically heals over several weeks to months as the implant integrates with the jawbone (osseointegration). Attend all follow-up appointments so your dentist can monitor healing and plan the next steps, including placing the abutment and crown.

## When to Contact Us

Call the clinic if you experience excessive bleeding, severe pain not relieved by medication, persistent swelling after several days, fever, or any unusual symptoms.
